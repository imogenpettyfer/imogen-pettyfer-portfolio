Cymbeline Gin

The same way the mind functions concerning creativity in great classical art is the same way it functions when you make creative breakthroughs in science.

Cymbeline Gin, named after the Shakespearian play was inspired by the character Imogen (or Innogen) which is where my name comes from. With the history behind that and never looking into the character before I found that this would be a great opportunity to learn more about my name while creating a project that I can be proud of. Surprised by what I found, it made for a great connection for the gin product I wanted to present.

With the name and idea now settled I wanted to keep a sophisticated and clean label. I spent the majority of my time working on the wording as it thought it was very important to have that included in the design. Then for the graphics I went outside to cut a few twigs which I then scanned to create a simple and delicate graphic. Once the writing and graphics were all flushed out, I Looked for typefaces to use. I ended up using Baskerville for the serif font (both semibold and regular) and for the cursive, I ended up drawing it by hand with inspiration from a script typeface called Fayette. Lastly I wanted to have a parchment paper background texture to suggest the age of the story Cymbeline but lighten the texture to keep the bottle from looking too old-fashioned.
